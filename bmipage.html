GENERAL FITNESS RATES

For a healthy workout, get your heart rate up into this target zone for at least 10 minutes at a time. You should be able to talk but not sing, and you should break a sweat. Try:

Walking as fast as you can

Riding your bike

Playing doubles tennis

Cutting the lawn with a push mower

Aim for a total of at least 150 minutes every week.

Pressed for time? You can get the same health gains in 75 minutes a week by bumping your heart rate into the upper end of your general fitness zone. Try:

Jogging

Aerobics or cycling class

Playing singles tennis

Heavy yard work, like shoveling

INTERVAL HEART RATES

Boost your exercise benefits with interval training.

High-intensity interval training, or HIIT, will help you burn calories and fat as well as build endurance and slow the aging process.

After you warm up for 5-10 minutes, alternate bursts of exercise in your high-intensity target zone with short recovery periods at around half of your maximum heart rate.

For example, run as fast as you can for 1 minute, then walk for 2 minutes. Do that five times for a great workout in only 15 minutes.

Mix up your workouts during the week.

High-intensity intervals: 2-3 times

General fitness: 3 times

If you have a medical condition, check with your doctor before you try pushing yourself hard.
